# Circle of Willis

The circle of Willis is a network of arteries located at the base of the brain. It connects the anterior and posterior cerebral circulations, providing an alternative pathway for blood flow to the brain in case of blockage in one of the main arteries.

It is formed by the following arteries:

[[Anterior communicating artery]]

[[Anterior cerebral artery]]

[[Internal carotid artery]]

[[Middle cerebral artery]]

[[Posterior communicating artery]]

[[Basilar artery]]
