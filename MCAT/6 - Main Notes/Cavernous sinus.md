# Cavernous sinus

The cavernous sinus is a pair of hollow spaces located under the brain, behind the eye sockets. They are a part of the brain's venous sinus system, and the contain important vessels and nerves that drain the face, eyes, and brain.

The sinuses are surrounded by the dura mater. The sinuses contain the internal carotid artery, cranial nerves, and the sympathetic plexus.
